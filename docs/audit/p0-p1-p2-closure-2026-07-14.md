An additional internal read-only review of the closure candidate found and fixed eight production
hardening gaps: a required SP1 job could previously skip its real proof steps on pull requests;
proof artifacts lacked a settlement-confirmed pruning lifecycle; queue permissions and capacity
were not enforced; build scripts could validate an ELF and later embed the mutable shared target
path; SettlementManager's hot owner could rewire dependencies or directly revert the latest
finalized head outside timelocked governance; ChainRegistry accepted undefined admission modes as
permissionless; ChainRegistry's controller remained mutable after governance lock; and production
deployment configured ChainRegistry governance without executing or verifying its irreversible
lock. This is
internal review evidence, not a substitute for the independent external review still required
before public production launch.
